PRecycling Project

Fraunhofer IVV hosted the 12-month consortium meeting of PRecycling Project on the 15th and 16th of March in Freising, Germany. During this meeting, PRecycling partners came together to discuss the project’s developments and results achieved in the first year.


R-NanoLab, serving as Scientific Coordinator and WP2 leader, presented the latest progress on recovering and converting recyclable materials into recyclates.


Specifically R-NanoLab is responsible on sampling and analysis of materials for recycling as well as Recycling/Upcycling and compounding of recyclates.

ROSiE Project

ROSiE General Assembly meeting hosted by Latvijas Universitate was completed in Riga, Latvia on the 22nd and 23rd of March. The meeting’s primary purpose was to discuss the initial results of ROSiE project and the opportunities and challenges associated with Open Science, as well as to plan for the next steps. Partners with different discipline backgrounds met again to exchange ideas and discuss ways to optimize the ROSiE project's outcomes and ensure its sustainability.


As leader of WP8-Communication and Dissemination of ROSiE project, R-NanoLab presented all the work completed during the first year of the project, including goals that have been achieved and ideas for the next phase. 


Overall, the meeting was an excellent opportunity for partners to collaborate and share insights to ensure the project’s continued success.

DeCOAT Project

DeCOAT project held its 51-month meeting in Nuremberg Germany on March 27th marking the final phase of the project. With most work packages nearing completion, the project is generating promising results and use cases.


Alongside with the meeting R-NanoLab was also at the European Coatings Show (ECS 2023) in Nuremberg Germany representing the DeCOAT Project on the 28th – 30th of March.


The demands placed on paint and coatings continue to grow, with requirements for aesthetics, sustainability, functionality and process-proof at an all-time high. The European Coatings gave the visitors the opportunity to stayup-to-date and to discuss the latest developments in pigments, additives, adhesive and raw materials, intermediates for construction chemicals as well as laborator yand production equipment, testing and measuring equipment, application and environmental protection and safety work.


In general the combination of DeCOAT project meeting and the European Coatings Show was a valuable opportunity to share ideas and discuss the latest development sin the field, as well as to showcase the project’s progress and outcomes.

iClimaBuilt Project

The 24-month Progress Meeting of the Horizon 2020 Project iClimaBuilt took place in Dresden, Germany on 19th and 20th of April. Hosted by project partner, representatives from the 27 partner organizations and project coordination team from NTUA attended the meeting and discussed the project’s progress and plans for the next period.

iClimaBuilt Consortium

iClimaBuilt’s goal is to create an open access ecosystem for developing, upscaling and testing innovations in building envelope materials and technical systems via its 9 Pilot Lines (PLs) to reach Nearly Zero Energy Buildings (nZEB) balance. At the same time, iClimaBuilt will support and help smallhigh-tech firms to scale up and cope with the continuous rising of technological complexity, assisting in the transformation of research results into innovations. During the meeting, project partners had the opportunity to have a tour of the CUBE living laboratory of Dresden University of Technology (TUD) partner and visit the project test cases, demonstrating the long-term monitoring and user perception.


R-NanoLab team this period will be focusing their efforts on the project’s Open Call, which will be launching in August 2023. The idea of this open call is to deploy a set of test cases where (group of) companies outside the project will be able to develop and test any of their technologies referring to materials for building envelopes. iClimaBuilt will offer the related services through its Services Arena. Each experimentation team needs to describe its lack in knowledge, the selected services and how the selected services are expected to close these knowledge gaps.

EuReComp Project

The 1st EuReComp Open Workshop 2023 was held on the 20th of April in Dresden, Germany. The workshop covered various topics related to composite recycling, including an EoL Composites Market Analysis, the R6 strategy within EuReComp.


Attendees had the opportunity to learn about the EuReComp-learning platform, Composite Recycling Technologies, and Solvolysis Technologies within EuReComp. They also got to visit Dresden University of Technology (TUD-ILK) facilities where they witnessed a Lightweight engineering Demonstration and participated in various sessions. It was a great chance for attendees to network and learn about the latest trends and technologies in composite recycling.

Mr. Dionisis Semitekolos presented the EuReComp project at the project’s 1st Workshop

EuReComp 1st Workshop Attendees

EuReComp 1st Workshop Round Table: Options for circularity and recycling of composites in different industrial sectors

Robert Böhm (Moderator) - HTWK, Volker Wagner - Tilia, Andreas Ulbricht - CG Rail GmbH, Wolfgang Machunze - Airbus, Kate Trompeta - NTUA

Following the workshop, the EuReComp project's 12-month meeting took place the next day, on the 21st of April, hosted by partner TUD. Representatives from 18 partners attended the meeting and discussed the project's status and the latest results. 


R-NanoLab's role within the project, besides the coordination, is handling the recycled carbon fibers through electropolymerization or by applying sizing on their surface. Additionally, solvolysis wastes will be utilized as precursors in a chemical vapor deposition reactor for the production of nanomaterials. Filament extrusion with recycled fibers will also be used to support the production of the demonstrator cases of the project.


Overall, the 1st EuReComp Open Workshop 2023 and the subsequent 12-month meeting were incredibly productive and informative. They provided attendees with valuable insights into composite recycling and showcased the exciting progress being made by the EuReComp project.

JEC World 2023

R-NanoLab, in collaboration with EsSENce Cost Action, participated in JEC World Composites 2023 in Paris from the 25th until the 27th of April, which provided an excellent opportunity for the team to connect with numerous companies in the industry. The event presented an invaluable opportunity for R-NanoLab to engage in meaningful conversations about the latest trends and advancements in the field of composites.

R-NanoLab Team showing the work of EsSENce, Cost action

R-NanoLab in Jec Word

R-NanoLab also presented several ongoing European projects and showcased their work, which garnered a positive response from attendees.
Throughout the event, Dr. George Kotsikos, Project Officer at the European Commission, recently delivered a presentation titled "Advance Lightweight Materials—A European Commission Prospective" at JEC World. During his presentation, he highlighted the importance of advanced lightweight materials in various industries and discussed three notable projects: Eurecomp, Repair3D and Suspens project. Moreover, a discussion session took place between Dr. Kotsikos and Dionysis Semitekolos about the Eurecomp project's potential to create digital passports for recycled materials. The idea behind this is to provide traceability and transparency in the supply chain, making it easier to verify the origin and quality of recycled materials.


Participating in the JEC World Composites event enabled R-NanoLab to expand its professional network, meet new people, and build relationships. The team left the event feeling inspired and motivated to continue their efforts to advance the field of composites. The team is eagerly looking forward to next year's event and the potential opportunities it may bring.

4th EMMC International Workshop

On April 27th, Professor Costas Charitidis from R-NanoLab chaired the 4th session on the second day of the EMMC International Workshop. The session was titled  "Characterization as accelerator on advanced physics-based modelling; from product development to Use-Cases" and explored the ways in which characterization can be used to accelerate the development of advanced physics-based models.


The importance of characterization as a tool for ensuring the accuracy of modeling was a key focus of the session, which also highlighted some of the use-cases where such models have been successfully deployed.

Professor Costas Charitidis from R-NanoLab chaired the 4th session on the second day of the EMMC International Workshop

Advanced Nanomechanical Testing for Accelerated Material Development by Verena Maier-Kiener (Montanuniversität Leoben, AT)

The session was framed within the context of WP5 of NanoMECommons project, which focused on standardization, collaboration with existing NMBP characterization projects, and EMCC engagement. 


Attendees of Session 4 gained valuable insights into the importance of characterization in the development of advanced physics-based models, as well as learning about the latest advances in this exciting field.
